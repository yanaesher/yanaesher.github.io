History of Chess

Chess is one of the oldest board games in the world, with a history spanning over 1500 years.

Origins in India

Chess originated in India as Chaturanga, a strategic game with elements of military tactics.

Evolution in Persia

The game was adapted in Persia and became known as Shatranj.

Chess in Europe

The game spread to Europe during the Middle Ages and was adapted into modern rules.

Modern Chess

Chess became part of global culture and sport, with the first World Chess Championship being held.

Why should you start playing chess?

LOGIC

Through playing chess, a person trains their thinking, learns to reason, and build cause-and-effect relationships. Chess is a great workout for the mind!

GOAL SETTING

Chess teaches perseverance, the ability to set and achieve goals, as well as planning the path to reach those goals.

CHARACTER AND WILLPOWER

Chess strengthens character and willpower. It also improves stress resistance, teaches how to accept defeat, and never give up!

INDEPENDENCE

By playing chess, a person learns to think independently and make decisions. Even the simplest choice, like the next move, teaches responsibility and self-reliance.

ATTENTION

A careless move in chess can lead to defeat, so during the game, we learn not to take our opponent off our radar, keep both our strategy and their moves in mind, and ask ourselves "Why did they make this move?"

CREATIVE THINKING

During chess lessons, we engage imagination and creativity. Often, after a chess session, students feel a surge of inspiration!
